§ 01 · CRISIS BRIEFING
§ 01

The Crisis — Full Situation Briefing

Assessment

What began as isolated militia raids has escalated into a systematic proxy campaign conclusively linked by Valdoria's intelligence services to state-level funding from Lapland. The scale, coordination, and resourcing of the militia activity is inconsistent with independent non-state actors — this is an organised operation.

The Ethnic Cover Story

All three groups publicly claim to fight for ethnic Laplandic minorities within Valdoria — a community of approximately 120,000 people who have lived in northern Valdoria for generations with full citizenship rights.

Valdoria's intelligence assessment: this is a cover story. Weapons recovered from militia operations include military-grade equipment consistent with state-sourced supply chains running through China's special economic zone in northern Lapland.

§ 02

China renews and expands rare earth mineral contracts covering 40% of northern Lapland. Agreement includes a bilateral non-interference clause. Valdoria files a formal note of concern — ignored.

§ 04 · CORE GRIEVANCES
§ 04

Core Grievances — On the Record

Scope
These are the specific injuries Valdoria intends to raise formally in the General Speakers List and Open Floor. Each has an evidential basis and a specific demand attached to it.
1
Unanswered Diplomatic Communications
Three formal diplomatic notes submitted over five months — all ignored or returned. Under the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations, Lapland's failure to respond constitutes a serious breach of basic state-to-state obligations. Valdoria demands a formal acknowledgement and response to all three notes as a precondition to any substantive dialogue.
2
40,000 Civilians Displaced Without Remedy
Forty thousand Valdorian citizens driven from their homes by militia activity directly linked to Lapland's state apparatus. Living in emergency facilities, many for over a year. No humanitarian assistance offered. Valdoria demands a jointly-funded humanitarian relief programme and a timeline for safe return.
3
Death of Four Border Police Officers
The December ambush in Velsk Province killed Officers Petro Balan, Dmytro Serko, Vasyl Marko, and Olena Yuts. They have names. They had families. Valdoria demands that this forum acknowledge these deaths as a result of state-sponsored proxy violence.
4
Economic Sabotage of Transit Corridors
Deliberate disruption of Valdoria's northern transit corridors has cost an estimated USD 18 million per month in lost revenue since November. Intelligence suggests militia targeting of the Velsk corridor was specifically instructed because it competes with Lapland's own southern transit route.
5
China's Role as a Shield
Valdoria is acutely aware that Lapland's impunity is underwritten by China's bilateral non-interference treaty with Lapland. This will be raised in private bilateral talks and signalled to the United States as a structural obstacle to any durable resolution.
§ 05 · POSITION & OPTIONS
§ 05

Position & Expected Outcomes

Valdoria's Standing Position

Valdoria stands as a sovereign state subjected to a systematic proxy campaign of destabilisation. Every available bilateral diplomatic remedy has been exhausted and met with silence, evasion, and contempt.

Invasion into Lapland is off the table. It would trigger international condemnation, invite sanctions, and risk a broader regional conflict Valdoria's armed forces cannot sustain beyond six weeks. Regime change in Lapland is not sought — only accountability.

Option A
Military Monitoring Force

A military joint force in collaboration with a humanitarian monitoring mission, effective for 30 days, securing Valdoria's borders and slowing escalation. Valdoria will accept new territories as a partial win and support a UN resolution letter. Prime Minister Voronov's coalition survives and grows.

⚠ WARNING — Highly unfavourable to China, Valdoria's largest trade partner. May trigger sanctions and escalation. A full-scale war backed by China and the USA would break out, throwing Valdoria into a 20-year conflict ending in stalemate with 40% civilian casualties and economic destruction.
Option B
Ceasefire & Peace Deal

A co-collaborative peace treaty and joint task force cracking down on militias and terrorist groups at Valdoria's borders. In 20 years, successful negotiations lead Valdoria to discover rare earth deposits in its mountains — long-term economic prosperity.

⚠ WARNING — Highly unfavourable to Valdoria's domestic constituency. May trigger state secession. Prime Minister Vladimir's coalition may be overthrown by militia forces.
§ 06 · MANDATE & RED LINES
§ 06

Mandate & Red Lines

Mandate 01 — Protect National Interest
Valdoria's economic treaties with Lapland — covering energy, agriculture, and transit corridors worth USD 2.1 billion annually — must not be formally severed. Any resolution language triggering automatic treaty suspension is unacceptable. The crisis must be resolved in a way that preserves the structural framework for economic normalisation.
Mandate 02 — Secure a Resolution That Names the Cause
The agreement must acknowledge that the displacement of Valdorian civilians is a direct consequence of armed militia activity. Valdoria will accept non-binding language on intervention. It will not accept language that is silent on causation.
Mandate 03 — Build the Coalition: United States First

The USA needs a signed outcome more than anyone. Give the United States flexibility on monitoring language in exchange for the USA holding the line on causation language in the resolution.

China is not a target for persuasion. Lapland is a secondary target — if their Foreign Minister shows signs of fracture, exploit it immediately through Kastelova in the bilateral space.

⛔
Red Line 1 — No Resolution Omitting Displaced Civilians
Any resolution failing to acknowledge the 40,000 displaced civilians as a central fact of the crisis is unacceptable. Valdoria will vote Against. This is the absolute floor below which no deal can be made.
⛔
Red Line 2 — No Resolution Exonerating Lapland
If the resolution characterises the situation as a mutual border dispute or implies equal responsibility, Valdoria will not sign. The resolution must at minimum be neutral on causation — it cannot actively reframe Valdoria as a co-contributor.
⛔
Red Line 3 — No Bilateral Framework Brokered Exclusively by China
Valdoria will not enter bilateral dialogue with Lapland in which China is the sole mediating party. China's economic interests in Lapland make it structurally incapable of neutral mediation. The mediating party must be the United States, the UN, or a jointly agreed neutral.
⛔
Red Line 5 — Valdoria Does Not Walk Out
No matter how frustrated, no matter how provocative Lapland's denials or China's procedural tactics — Valdoria does not walk out. Walking out hands the moral victory to Lapland and allows them to characterise Valdoria as unwilling to engage.

The Intercept — Classified Signals Intelligence

Valdoria's signals intelligence unit has intercepted partial communications between officials in Lapland's Interior Ministry referencing the authorisation of two funding tranches to militia networks operating inside Valdoria's border provinces. The intercepts reference code names, not Deputy Minister Halonen by name.

The evidence is credible but not airtight.

Total intercepted transfers: USD 14 million, routed through shell companies in China's special economic zone in northern Lapland.

Decision Framework — When to Reveal
  • If the resolution looks like it will pass with Lapland named as responsible — hold the intelligence. It is not needed.
  • If the resolution is being hollowed out by China's amendments and Lapland looks like they will walk away clean — reveal it. This is the last available lever.
  • Only Prime Minister Voronov may authorise disclosure. Col. Harken advises. No one else decides.
⚠ LAST RESORT — The intercept is Valdoria's only covert leverage. Deploying it prematurely eliminates the element of surprise. Hold until the forum is on the verge of producing a resolution that exonerates Lapland entirely.
