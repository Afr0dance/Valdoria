§ 01

The Crisis — Full Situation

Assessment
What began as isolated militia raids has escalated into systematic proxy campaign conclusively linked to state-level funding from Lapland. Scale, coordination, and resourcing is inconsistent with independent non-state actors — this is an organized operation.
Korra Province — Northern Border
Population: 180,000 | Displaced: 14,000
Heaviest militia activity. Three villages completely evacuated. Militia operate freely at night. Regional capital Korrava housing evacuees at 340% capacity.
Velsk Province — Northeast Border
Population: 220,000 | Displaced: 18,000
Most economically significant crossing disrupted. Four border officers killed in December ambush. Velsk-Lapland corridor now compromised.
Dravan Province — Northwest Border
Population: 95,000 | Displaced: 8,000
Lowest intensity but growing. Credible intelligence suggests third militia cell assembling in Lapland-adjacent forest regions.
§ 02

Core Grievances

1
Unanswered Diplomatic Communications
Three formal diplomatic notes submitted over five months — all ignored or returned. Lapland's failure to respond is serious breach of Vienna Convention. Valdoria demands formal acknowledgement and response as precondition to dialogue.
2
40,000 Civilians Displaced Without Remedy
Valdorian citizens driven from homes by militia directly linked to Lapland's state apparatus. Living in emergency facilities, many over a year. Valdoria demands jointly-funded humanitarian relief and safe return timeline.
3
Death of Four Border Officers
December ambush killed Officers Petro Balan, Dmytro Serko, Vasyl Marko, Olena Yuts. They have names. They had families. Valdoria demands forum acknowledge these deaths as result of state-sponsored proxy violence.
4
Economic Sabotage of Transit Corridors
Deliberate disruption of northern transit corridors cost USD 18 million monthly since November. Intelligence suggests militia targeting was specifically instructed because it competes with Lapland's own transit route.
§ 03

Position & Expected Outcomes

Valdoria's Standing

Invasion into Lapland is off the table. Would trigger international condemnation, sanctions, and broader regional conflict Valdoria cannot sustain beyond 6 weeks.

Regime change is not sought. Only accountability — formal acknowledgement that Lapland's conduct caused civilian displacement and officer deaths.

Option A: Military Monitoring Force
Joint military force with humanitarian monitoring for 30 days securing borders and slowing escalation. Valdoria accepts as partial win. PM coalition survives and grows.
Option B: Ceasefire & Peace Deal
Co-collaborative peace treaty and joint task force cracking down on militias at borders. In 20 years, successful negotiations lead Valdoria to discover rare earth deposits and economic prosperity.
§ 04

Mandate & Red Lines

Mandate 01
Valdoria's economic treaties with Lapland (USD 2.1B annually) must not be formally severed. Resolution must preserve framework for economic normalization once proxy campaign ceases.
Mandate 02
Resolution must acknowledge civilian displacement is direct consequence of armed militia activity. Valdoria accepts non-binding intervention language but will not accept silence on causation.
Mandate 03
Build coalition with United States first. USA needs signed outcome more than anyone. Give USA flexibility on monitoring in exchange for USA holding line on causation language.
