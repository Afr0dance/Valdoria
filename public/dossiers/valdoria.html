Economy & Vulnerabilities
  • Valdoria's economy is heavily dependent on three transit corridors running through its northern border provinces into Lapland — accounting for 31% of total national export revenue.
  • These corridors are now partially disrupted by militia activity. Two border checkpoints have been closed since November, costing an estimated USD 18 million per month in lost transit fees.

§ 2 · THE CRISIS
§ 2

The Crisis — Full Situation Briefing

What began as isolated militia raids in Valdoria's border provinces has escalated into a systematic proxy campaign that Valdoria's intelligence services have conclusively linked to state-level funding from Lapland. The scale, coordination, and resourcing of the militia activity is inconsistent with independent non-state actors — this is an organised operation.
Korra Province
NORTHERN BORDER · POP. 180,000
14,000
civilians displaced
Heaviest militia activity. Three villages — Ustava, Merin, and Polach — have been completely evacuated. Militia groups operate freely at night, burning farmland and blocking roads. 14,000 civilians displaced from this province alone. Most are sheltering in the regional capital Korrava, 60km south, in temporary facilities at 340% capacity.
Velsk Province
NORTHEAST BORDER · POP. 220,000
18,000
civilians displaced
Militia groups here have disrupted the Velsk-Lapland transit corridor — the most economically significant of the three border crossings. 18,000 civilians displaced. Four Valdorian border police officers killed in an ambush in December. No group has claimed responsibility.
Dravan Province
NORTHWEST BORDER · POP. 95,000
8,000
civilians displaced
Lowest militia intensity but growing. Credible intelligence suggests a third militia cell is being assembled in the Lapland-adjacent forest regions of Dravan. 8,000 civilians displaced. Local government has declared a state of emergency. Valdoria's national guard has been deployed but cannot cross the border in pursuit.
The Militia Groups

Three distinct groups operate across the three provinces:

  • Korra Liberation Front (KLF) — Korra Province · Claims rights of ethnic Laplandic minorities · Leadership traced to Lapland's Interior Ministry operatives
  • Northern Shield Brigade (NSB) — Velsk Province · Conducted Velsk ambush · Weapons consistent with state-sourced supply chains
  • Velsk Sovereignty Movement (VSM) — Velsk/Dravan · No significant recruitment from actual Laplandic-Valdorian communities

All three publicly claim to fight for the rights of ethnic Laplandic minorities within Valdoria — a community of approximately 120,000 people who have lived in northern Valdoria for generations with full citizenship rights. Valdoria's intelligence assessment: this is a cover story. Weapons recovered include military-grade equipment consistent with state-sourced supply chains running through China's special economic zone in northern Lapland.

§ 3 · CORE GRIEVANCES
§ 3

Core Grievances — On the Record

Scope
These are the specific injuries Valdoria intends to raise formally in the General Speakers List and Open Floor. Each has an evidential basis and a specific demand attached to it.
1
Unanswered Diplomatic Communications
Three formal diplomatic notes submitted over five months. All ignored or returned. Under the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations, Lapland's failure to respond constitutes a serious breach of basic state-to-state obligations. Valdoria demands a formal acknowledgement and response to all three notes as a precondition to any substantive dialogue.
2
40,000 Civilians Displaced Without Remedy
Forty thousand Valdorian citizens have been driven from their homes by militia activity directly linked to Lapland's state apparatus. These citizens have been living in emergency facilities, many for over a year. No humanitarian assistance has been offered by Lapland. Valdoria demands a jointly-funded humanitarian relief programme and a timeline for safe return.
3
Death of Four Border Police Officers
The December ambush in Velsk Province killed Officers Petro Balan, Dmytro Serko, Vasyl Marko, and Olena Yuts. They have names. They had families. Valdoria demands that this forum acknowledge these deaths as a result of state-sponsored proxy violence.
4
Economic Sabotage of Transit Corridors
The deliberate disruption of Valdoria's northern transit corridors has cost an estimated USD 18 million per month in lost revenue since November. Intelligence suggests militia targeting of the Velsk corridor was specifically instructed because it competes with Lapland's own southern transit route.
§ 4 · POSITION
§ 4

Position

Valdoria stands as a sovereign state that has been subjected to a systematic proxy campaign of destabilisation by its neighbour, Lapland — conducted through the covert funding of armed militia groups operating inside Valdorian territory.

  • You do not seek war. Invasion into Lapland is off the table. It would trigger international condemnation, invite sanctions, and risk a broader regional conflict Valdoria's armed forces cannot sustain beyond six weeks.
  • You do not seek regime change in Lapland. Valdoria seeks accountability — a formal international acknowledgement that Lapland's conduct caused the displacement of 40,000 Valdorian civilians and the deaths of four border officers.
Option A
Military Monitoring Force
A military joint force in collaboration with a humanitarian monitoring mission, effective for 30 days, securing Valdoria's borders and possibly slowing down escalation review and militia activities.

Valdoria will accept new territories if this option is chosen as a partial win, Valdoria will vote on a support resolution letter in the UN. Prime Minister Valdoria's coalition will survive and grow in favor.
⚠ WARNING: Choosing this option is highly unfavourable to Valdoria's largest trade partner: China. This may trigger sanctions and escalation of wars. A full-scale war would break out with the backing of China and United States of America. Valdoria would be thrown into a 20-year conflict which will end in a stalemate but a serious affection of 40% of Valdoria's civilian casualties in return and Valdoria's economy would be ultimately destroyed.
Option B
Ceasefire & Peace Deal
A co-collaborative peace treaty and task force will be proposed during this Special session. This would entail a joint task force which would crack down on militias and terrorist groups at the borders of Valdoria.

In 20 years, the people of Valdoria would enjoy increased trade as the successful negotiations have led Valdoria to discover rare earth deposits in its mountains.
⚠ WARNING: Choosing this option is highly unfavourable to the people and constituents of Valdoria. This may trigger state secession from Valdoria. Prime Minister Vladimir's coalition may be overthrown by militia forces.
§ 5 · MANDATE
§ 5

Mandate & Red Lines

The following instructions have been issued by Prime Minister Vladimir. All four delegation members are bound by them. Any deviation requires direct authorisation from the Head of Delegation.

Protect National Interest
Valdoria's economic treaties with Lapland — covering energy, agriculture, and transit corridors worth USD 2.1 billion annually — must not be formally severed as a consequence of this forum. Any resolution language that would trigger automatic treaty suspension is unacceptable. The crisis must be resolved in a way that preserves the structural framework for economic normalisation once Lapland ceases its proxy campaign.
Secure a Resolution That Names the Cause
The agreement must, at minimum, acknowledge that the displacement of Valdorian civilians is a direct consequence of armed militia activity in the border provinces. Lapland's failure to respond to three diplomatic communications is a documented fact of this crisis. Valdoria will accept non-binding language on intervention. It will not accept language that is silent on causation.
Build the Coalition — United States First

As Valdoria's bilateral priority is the United States. The USA needs a signed outcome more than anyone. Your Ally is USA — give the United States flexibility on monitoring language in exchange for the USA holding the line on causation language in the resolution.

China is not a target for persuasion. Lapland is a secondary target — if their Foreign Minister shows signs of fracture, exploit it immediately through Kastelova in the bilateral space.

⛔ Red Line 1 — No Resolution Omitting Displaced Civilians
Any resolution failing to acknowledge the 40,000 displaced civilians as a central fact of the crisis is unacceptable. Valdoria will vote Against. This is the absolute floor below which no deal can be made.
⛔ Red Line 2 — No Resolution Exonerating Lapland
If the resolution characterises the situation as a mutual border dispute or implies equal responsibility, Valdoria will not sign. The resolution must at minimum be neutral on causation — it cannot actively reframe Valdoria as a co-contributor.
⛔ Red Line 3 — No Bilateral Framework Brokered Exclusively by China
Valdoria will not enter bilateral dialogue with Lapland in which China is the sole mediating party. China's economic interests in Lapland make it structurally incapable of neutral mediation. The mediating party must be the United States, the UN, or a jointly agreed neutral.
⛔ Red Line 4 — Valdoria Does Not Walk Out
No matter how frustrated, no matter how provocative Lapland's denials or China's procedural tactics — Valdoria does not walk out. Walking out hands the moral victory to Lapland and allows them to characterise Valdoria as unwilling to engage.

§ 10

Secret Intelligence — Eyes Only

Classified — Valdoria Intelligence File

Valdoria's signals intelligence unit has intercepted partial communications between officials in Lapland's Interior Ministry referencing the authorisation of two funding tranches to militia networks operating inside Valdoria's border provinces. The intercepts reference code names, not the official (Deputy Minister Halonen) by name. The evidence is credible but not airtight.

Total intercepted transfers: USD 14 million, routed through shell companies in China's special economic zone in northern Lapland.

Decision Framework — When to Reveal
  • If the resolution looks like it will pass with Lapland named as responsible — hold the intelligence. It is not needed.
  • If the resolution is being hollowed out by China's amendments and Lapland looks like they will walk away clean — reveal it. This is the last available lever.
  • Only Prime Minister Voronov may authorise disclosure. Col. Harken advises. No one else decides.
⚠ LAST RESORT — The intercept is Valdoria's only covert leverage. Deploying it prematurely eliminates the element of surprise. Hold until the forum is on the verge of producing a resolution that exonerates Lapland entirely.
